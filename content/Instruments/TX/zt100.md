## Overview

The ZT-100B is part of Zonge’s new rack mount transmitter system rated for outputs of 100A and 1200V. It represents a major change from the GGT series because it does not require a 400 Hz generator and instead will use a readily available 50 or 60 hz generator. The ZT-100B switching unit is connected to a switched-mode power supply (SMPS) which Zonge can provide and mount in a rugged case along with the ZT-100B switching unit.
Customers can choose their power supply but Zonge will offer at least a few options that we will bundle and mount with the ZT-100B.

- A low power SMPS that takes single phase 110V-120V as input for maximum portability in the US, Canada, Mexico, or wherever one can obtain a low voltage generator.
- A medium power SMPS that takes single phase 240V as input. This power supply will be called the ZPS12-1200. This will replace the GGT-10 and even exceed some of its output specifications (link to datasheet below).
- A high power SMPS that takes 3-phase power as input from a larger industrial style generator.

## FAQ

**Does the ZT-100B hookup directly to a generator?**

No, a SMPS is required, we would love to talk to you about your survey needs and anticipated use cases to figure out the right power supply for you.

**Is the ZT-100B compatible with my old Zonge generator?**

No, it is not because the old Zonge generator’s are 400 hz and it is unlikely that there is a suitable SMPS on the market compatible with 3-phase 400 hz as input. The good thing is that 50 and 60 hz generators are readily available and it is usually even possible to rent one locally.

**Will the transmitter be GPS synchronized with a GDP, ZEN or other geophysical receiver that use GPS sync?**

For the ZEN and GDP the answer is yes. For most other receivers the answer is likely also yes especially for 1 Hz and higher.
